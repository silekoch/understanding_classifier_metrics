How To Read This Playground

        

          The key chain is: threshold on distributions -> confusion matrix counts -> rates (TPR/FPR/precision/recall)
          -> points on ROC and PR curves.
        

        
Interpolation overlays are optional in the advanced section so the default view stays focused on empirical behavior.

        
Simple examples that yield different curve shapes:

        
    
          
  • Large mean gap, similar variances: strong top-left belly and high AUC.
    • 
          
  • Small mean gap: curve close to the diagonal baseline.
    • 
          
  • Unequal variances: asymmetric curvature and slope changes.
    • 
          
  • Outliers in the positive class: visible kink/shoulder in empirical ROC.
