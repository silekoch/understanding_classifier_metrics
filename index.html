What Is Better Than a Triangle?

        

          A triangle uses only three points, so it ignores shape information. Better choices are a concave hull
          interpolation (non-parametric, data-driven) or a Gaussian model ROC (parametric, smooth "belly").
        

        
Simple examples that yield different ROC shapes:

        
    
          
  • Large mean gap, similar variances: strong top-left belly and high AUC.
    • 
          
  • Small mean gap: curve close to the diagonal baseline.
    • 
          
  • Unequal variances: asymmetric curvature and slope changes.
    • 
          
  • Outliers in the positive class: visible kink/shoulder in empirical ROC.
